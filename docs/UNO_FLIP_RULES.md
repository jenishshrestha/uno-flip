## 2. Setup

1. **Players:** 2-10 players.
2. **Determine dealer:** Each player draws a card. The player with the highest number on the Light Side becomes the dealer.
3. **Deal:** Dealer shuffles and deals **7 cards** to each player, one at a time.
4. **Card orientation:** Players hold cards with the **Light Side facing them** and the Dark Side facing outward (visible to opponents, but opponents only see the uniform dark back).
5. **Draw pile:** Place the remaining deck face-down with the **Light Side facing down** (Dark Side up).
6. **Discard pile:** Flip the top card of the draw pile over to start the discard pile, Light Side face-up.
7. **Play always begins on the Light Side.**
8. **First player:** The player to the **left of the dealer** goes first.
9. **Direction:** Play proceeds **clockwise** initially.

### If the First Discard Card Is an Action/Wild Card

| First Card | What Happens |
|---|---|
| **Draw One** | First player (left of dealer) draws 1 card and loses their turn. |
| **Skip** | First player is skipped; the second player goes first. |
| **Reverse** | Dealer plays first instead; play direction becomes counter-clockwise. |
| **Flip** | Everything flips to the Dark Side immediately. First player must match the revealed Dark Side of the Flip card. |
| **Wild** | First player (left of dealer) chooses a color, then plays. |
| **Wild Draw Two** | Return it to the draw pile, reshuffle, and flip a new card. |

## 3. Light Side Cards and Effects

### Number Cards (1-9)
- Play on a matching **color** or matching **number**.
- Worth their face value in scoring.

### Draw One
- The **next player draws 1 card** from the draw pile and **loses their turn**.
- Can only be played on a matching color or on another Draw One card.

### Skip
- The **next player loses their turn**.
- Can only be played on a matching color or on another Skip card.

### Reverse
- **Reverses the direction** of play (clockwise becomes counter-clockwise, and vice versa).
- Can only be played on a matching color or on another Reverse card.

### Flip
- When played, **all cards in the game flip to the Dark Side** (see Section 5 for full details).
- Can only be played on a matching color or on another Flip card.

### Wild
- Can be played **at any time**, regardless of the card on the discard pile.
- The player **chooses any color** to continue play.
- The next player must match the chosen color (or play a Wild).

### Wild Draw Two
- The player **chooses a color**, and the next player must **draw 2 cards** and **lose their turn**.
- **Restriction:** May only be legally played if the player has **no cards matching the current discard color** in their hand. (Having a matching number or symbol is acceptable -- only color matters for legality.)
- **Can be challenged** (see Section 6).

## 4. Dark Side Cards and Effects

### Number Cards (1-9)
- Same matching rules as Light Side: match by color or number.
- Worth face value in scoring.

### Draw Five
- The **next player draws 5 cards** from the draw pile and **loses their turn**.
- Can only be played on a matching color or on another Draw Five card.

### Reverse
- Works identically to the Light Side Reverse.
- Reverses play direction.

### Skip Everyone
- **ALL other players are skipped.** The player who played this card **gets another turn immediately.**
- Can only be played on a matching color or on another Skip Everyone card (regardless of color).
- Yes, this skips every single other player in the game, not just the next one.

### Flip
- When played, **all cards in the game flip to the Light Side** (see Section 5).
- Can only be played on a matching color or on another Flip card.

### Wild
- Works identically to the Light Side Wild.
- Player chooses any color.

### Wild Draw Color
- The player **chooses a color**. The next player must **draw cards from the draw pile until they draw a card of the chosen color**, then they **lose their turn**.
- There is no cap on how many cards they may have to draw. They keep drawing until a card of the named color appears.
- **Restriction:** May only be legally played if the player has **no cards matching the current discard color**. (Having a matching number/symbol is fine.)
- **Can be challenged** (see Section 7).

## 5. The Flip Mechanic

When a Flip card is played, the following happens **in this order:**

1. **Discard pile:** The entire discard pile is flipped over. The Flip card that was just played ends up on the **bottom** of the discard pile. The new top card (previously the bottom of the discard pile) becomes the active discard, now showing its opposite side.
2. **Draw pile:** The entire draw pile is flipped over, revealing the opposite side.
3. **All players' hands:** Every player flips all their cards to show the opposite side.

**Key points:**
- If the game was on the Light Side, it switches to the Dark Side (and vice versa).
- Play continues on the new side until another Flip card is played.
- After flipping, the next player must match the **new top card** of the discard pile on the **new active side**.
- The Flip card itself counts as a played card -- play passes to the next player after the flip occurs (unless the game state says otherwise).

## 6. Wild Draw Two Rules (Light Side)

### When Can It Be Played?
- Only when the player has **no cards matching the current discard pile color**.
- The player MAY have cards matching the number or symbol -- that is allowed.

### Effect
- Player names a new color.
- Next player draws 2 cards and loses their turn.

### Challenge Mechanic
The next player (the one who would draw) **can challenge** the Wild Draw Two play:

1. The challenged player must **show their hand** to the challenger.
2. **If the challenge is successful** (the player DID have a matching color card -- illegal play):
   - The player who played the Wild Draw Two must **draw 2 cards** instead.
   - The challenger does NOT draw and takes their turn normally.
3. **If the challenge fails** (the player had NO matching color card -- legal play):
   - The challenger must draw the original **2 cards PLUS 2 additional penalty cards** (4 cards total).
   - The challenger loses their turn.

---

## 7. Wild Draw Color Rules (Dark Side)

### When Can It Be Played?
- Only when the player has **no cards matching the current discard pile color**.

### Effect
- Player names a color.
- Next player **draws cards one at a time from the draw pile until they draw a card of the named color**.
- That player then **loses their turn**.
- There is no maximum -- the player could theoretically draw many cards.

### Challenge Mechanic
The next player **can challenge** the Wild Draw Color play:

1. The challenged player must **show their hand** to the challenger.
2. **If the challenge is successful** (illegal play -- player had a matching color):
   - The player who played the Wild Draw Color must **draw cards until they get the named color** instead.
   - The challenger does NOT draw and takes their turn normally.
3. **If the challenge fails** (legal play -- player had no matching color):
   - The challenger must **draw cards until they get the named color PLUS 2 additional penalty cards**.
   - The challenger loses their turn.

## 8. Skip Everyone (Dark Side)

- **Skips ALL other players** in the game, regardless of how many players there are.
- The player who played Skip Everyone **immediately gets another turn**.
- In a 2-player game, this functions the same as a regular Skip (the opponent is skipped, you go again).
- In a 3+ player game, every other player is skipped -- not just the next one.
- Can be played on a matching color or on any other Skip Everyone card.

---

## 9. UNO Calling Rules

- A player must shout **"UNO"** when they play their **second-to-last card** (going down to 1 card remaining).
- The call must happen **before the next player begins their turn**.
- **If caught not calling UNO:** The player must **draw 2 penalty cards**.
- **Any other player** can catch the offender and call them out.
- If no one catches the player before the next player's turn begins, the penalty does not apply.
- If the player is forced to draw cards (and goes above 1 card), they no longer need to maintain the UNO call.

## 10. Scoring

When a player goes out (plays their last card), they score points based on the cards remaining in **all other players' hands**. Points are counted based on **whichever side the game ended on** (Light or Dark).

### Point Values

| Card | Points |
|---|---|
| Number cards (1-9) | Face value (1-9 points) |
| Draw One | 10 |
| Draw Five | 20 |
| Reverse | 20 |
| Skip | 20 |
| Flip | 20 |
| Skip Everyone | 30 |
| Wild | 40 |
| Wild Draw Two | 50 |
| Wild Draw Color | 60 |

**Scoring note:** You only count the cards on the side that the game ended on. If the game ended on the Dark Side, you count Dark Side values (Draw Five, Skip Everyone, Wild Draw Color, etc.). If it ended on the Light Side, you count Light Side values (Draw One, Skip, Wild Draw Two, etc.).

---

## 11. Winning

### Round Victory
- The first player to **play all their cards** wins the round.
- If the last card played is a Draw, Wild Draw, Skip, or other action card, its effect **still applies** to the next player before the round ends and scoring occurs.

### Game Victory
- The first player to accumulate **500 or more points** across multiple rounds wins the game.
- **Alternative scoring:** Players can agree to track points negatively -- the player with the **lowest score** when someone reaches 500 is declared the winner.

## 12. Two-Player Special Rules

UNO Flip follows the same 2-player conventions as standard UNO:

| Card | 2-Player Effect |
|---|---|
| **Reverse** | Acts as a **Skip** -- the player who played it gets another turn. |
| **Skip** | The opponent is skipped; the player who played it gets another turn. |
| **Skip Everyone** | Same effect as Skip in 2-player (opponent is skipped, you go again). |
| **Draw One / Draw Five** | Opponent draws and loses their turn; you go again. |
| **Wild Draw Two / Wild Draw Color** | Opponent draws and loses their turn; you go again. |

In a 2-player game, any card that causes the other player to lose their turn effectively gives you consecutive turns.

## 13. Edge Cases

### Draw Pile Is Empty
- When the draw pile runs out, take the discard pile (except the top card), **shuffle it**, and place it face-down as the new draw pile.
- The current top card of the discard pile remains in place.
- Maintain the current side (Light or Dark) when reshuffling.

### First Card Is a Special Card
- See the table in Section 2 for each scenario.
- If the first card is a **Wild Draw Two**, it is buried back in the draw pile and a new card is flipped.

### Last Card Is an Action Card
- A player **can** end the game by playing an action card (Draw One, Skip, Wild Draw Two, etc.).
- The action still takes effect (e.g., if you go out with a Draw One, the next player still draws 1 card before scoring).

### Last Card Is a Wild Draw Color
- If the final card is a Wild Draw Color, the next player must still draw until they get the named color before the round ends and scoring begins.

### Game Ends on Dark Side
- Yes, a round can end while on the Dark Side. Score using Dark Side card values.

### Flip Card as Last Card
- If a Flip card is the last card played, everything flips to the other side, and scoring uses the new side's values for remaining cards.

---

## 14. Turn Order

- Play begins moving **clockwise** from the dealer.
- A **Reverse card** switches direction (clockwise to counter-clockwise, or vice versa).
- Direction changes persist until another Reverse card is played.
- In a 2-player game, direction is irrelevant (Reverse acts as Skip).

---

## 15. Stacking Rules

**Stacking is NOT allowed in official UNO Flip rules.**

- You **cannot** play a Draw One on top of another Draw One to force the next player to draw 2.
- You **cannot** stack Draw Five cards.
- You **cannot** stack Wild Draw Two or Wild Draw Color cards.
- Each draw card's effect resolves immediately against the next player. That player draws the required cards, loses their turn, and play moves on.

---

## 16. Challenge Rules Summary

Only **Wild Draw Two** (Light Side) and **Wild Draw Color** (Dark Side) can be challenged.

### General Challenge Process
1. The targeted player (who would have to draw) may choose to challenge.
2. The player who played the Wild card must reveal their hand.
3. The question is: **Did the player have any card matching the CURRENT discard pile color?**
   - If yes: The play was **illegal**. Challenge succeeds.
   - If no: The play was **legal**. Challenge fails.

### Challenge Outcomes

| Scenario | Wild Draw Two (Light) | Wild Draw Color (Dark) |
|---|---|---|
| **Challenge succeeds** (illegal play) | Offender draws 2 cards; challenger plays normally | Offender draws until they get the named color; challenger plays normally |
| **Challenge fails** (legal play) | Challenger draws 2 + 2 = **4 cards** total, loses turn | Challenger draws until named color + **2 extra cards**, loses turn |

Regular action cards (Draw One, Draw Five, Skip, Reverse, etc.) **cannot** be challenged.
